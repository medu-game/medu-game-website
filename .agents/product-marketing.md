## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Het game-based leerplatform voor de zorg — oefen klinisch redeneren in realistische 3D-scenario's, in de browser én als app. *(EN: The game-based learning platform for healthcare — practise clinical reasoning in realistic 3D scenarios, in the browser and as an app.)*

**What it does:** Medu.game turns clinical guidelines into playable 3D patient scenarios where healthcare professionals make decisions, complete tasks, and get instant feedback on every action. It sits deliberately *between theory and practice* — learners prepare safely and repeatably online, so scarce, expensive hands-on practice hours go further. Content is co-created with subject-matter experts and validated against current guidelines.

**Product category:** Game-based / serious-gaming learning platform for healthcare education (clinical reasoning & skills training). The "shelf": medical e-learning / serious games / simulation training for zorginstellingen.

**Product type:** B2B SaaS platform + content (modules). Playable standalone in Medu's own learning environment, or embedded in the customer's LMS via LTI.

**Business model:** Per user, per year. Custom quote based on modules chosen, number of licences, and whether the customer uses Medu's learning platform or their own LMS. Sales-led — primary conversion is a 30-min demo.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Healthcare organisations and training providers — ziekenhuizen, zorginstellingen, opleidingsinstituten, beroepsverenigingen, and BHV/EHBO/vakbekwaamheid training organisations. Active in multiple countries (NL core).

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Theory is cheap but passive and quickly forgotten; real practice hours are scarce and expensive. Professionals show up to practice days under-prepared, so costly hands-on time is spent on the basics — and mandatory continuing education often stalls on low engagement and poor retention.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Books / e-learning: onmisbare basis, maar passief en snel weggezakt ("droge kost").
- Classroom / simulation centres: onmisbaar maar schaars, duur en op locatie.
- Generic game-based / corporate skills tools: not healthcare-specific, no clinical casuïstiek.
- VR/hardware sim: hardware-bound, not mobile, hard to scale.

**What it costs them:** Wasted expensive practice hours, weak knowledge retention, hard-to-prove compliance/competence, limited ability to rehearse rare emergencies.

**Emotional tension:** Trainers frustrated that learners forget; professionals anxious facing rare high-stakes situations they've rarely practised; managers under pressure to show measurable, compliant training on a tight budget.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other game-based / serious-gaming learning vendors — fall short because they're generic skills tools, not built with the healthcare profession or its guidelines.
**Secondary:** Traditional e-learning/LMS content (passive, low retention) and on-location simulation/classroom training (scarce, expensive, not repeatable). VR med-sim (hardware-bound, not mobile/scalable).
**Indirect:** Doing nothing / box-ticking mandatory e-learning / books — cheapest path, but the knowledge doesn't transfer to practice.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Zorg-specifiek** — clinical content & casuïstiek for the medical practice, not a generic skills tool.
- **Co-creatie met de beroepsgroep** — scenarios built with subject-matter experts and validated against current guidelines.
- **Immersief én mobiel** — realistic 3D simulation in browser *and* app; play anywhere, progress travels along.
- **Lands in your LMS** — LTI integration with Moodle/Canvas/aNewSpring + SSO, or use Medu's own platform.
- **Meertalig & bewezen** — active in multiple countries; NL, EN, pt-BR; new languages easy to add.

**How we do it differently / why it's better:** Medu sits *between* theory and practice as a safe, repeatable, no-per-use-cost rehearsal layer — "wie eerst veilig de online simulatie speelt, komt bekwamer de praktijk in." You learn by doing, so it sticks: "je onthoudt wat je dóet."

**Why customers choose us:** The healthcare-specific choice — built *with* the profession, immersive yet mobile, and ready to land in their existing learning environment.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Mandatory training stalls; poor retention; scarce/expensive practice days spent on basics; no way to measure competence.
**Pull:** Safe, repeatable, realistic practice; mobile; measurable; fits the existing LMS; built with the profession.
**Habit:** "We've always done it in-person / via e-learning"; existing LMS content and classroom routine.
**Anxiety:** Integration effort, will staff actually use it, is the content a fit for our specialty, budget approval.
